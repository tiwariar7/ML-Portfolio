About This Project


Enhanced Heart Disease Risk Classification Model


Dataset Overview and Preprocessing


This project reimagines heart disease risk prediction by transforming raw medical data into actionable insights. The dataset underwent rigorous cleaning, including the removal of outliers and the normalization of key features like blood pressure, weight, and height. Advanced clustering techniques, like K-Modes, enabled separation of patterns by gender, revealing distinct health profiles for males and females.


Feature Engineering and Target Encoding


The dataset was enhanced with calculated metrics such as Body Mass Index (BMI) and Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP), which were categorized into meaningful groups. Additionally, age was segmented into cohorts to better reflect risk stratification. Multi-class target encoding expanded the scope of prediction, enabling classification into five distinct categories:


  • Low Risk
  • Coronary Artery Disease
  • Hypertension-Related Disease
  • Diabetes-Related Disease
  • Multiple Risk Factors


These enhancements enriched the dataset, ensuring a robust foundation for model training.


Data Visualization


Data visualization played a pivotal role in uncovering trends and correlations within the dataset. Seaborn and Matplotlib were utilized to present clear, compelling visuals. For instance:



  • Heatmaps showcased correlations among risk factors.
  • Bar charts highlighted the prevalence of various disease types.
  • ROC curves provided a visual evaluation of model performance, emphasizing reliability.




Model Training


A suite of advanced machine learning algorithms, including Decision Trees, Random Forests, MLP (Multi-Layer Perceptron), and XGBoost, was trained on the preprocessed data. Each model was fine-tuned with hyperparameter optimization to ensure peak performance. Notably, Random Forest emerged as a standout model following rigorous GridSearchCV optimization.



Evaluation Metrics



To ensure accurate predictions, multiple evaluation metrics were analyzed:


  • Accuracy: Measured overall correctness.
  • Precision: Evaluated true positive predictions.
  • Recall: Highlighted the model’s sensitivity.
  • F1 Score: Balanced precision and recall.
  • AUC (Area Under the Curve): Provided insight into the model’s ability to distinguish between classes.


A comprehensive confusion matrix and classification report confirmed the effectiveness of the chosen models in accurately categorizing heart disease risk levels.


Real-Time Prediction


The project includes an interactive prediction system that allows users to input personal health data to receive real-time risk assessments. This feature bridges the gap between theoretical modeling and practical application, empowering individuals with personalized insights into their heart health.
